Four elements of a myth:
1. The Moon The cycle, change, transformation, the first victim. "The first place where steam power came into practical use was in replacing one of the most brutal forms of human or animal labour: pumping of water out of mines." The Industrial Revolution & the creation of industrial traditions. Abolition of slavery supported economically by the integration of machines into industries.
2. The Beast The animal, the wolf, the savage, the monster. "[Fear of being replaced. A myth that is technological rather than biological, posthuman. Artificial Intelligence.]" A monstruous identity is created for technology. People are generally uncomfortable with the unknown. Machines carrying out more of the cruelty, so that people can afford respect.
3. The Transgression The sin, stigma, consequence, perversion. "The Greeks regarded the act of discovering fire with very split emotions. On the one hand, fire was for them as for us a great benefit to all humanity. On the other, the carrying down of fire from heaven to earth was a defiance of the Gods of Olympus, and could not but be punished by them as a piece of insolence towards their prerogatives, thus we see the great figure of Prometheus, the fire-bearer, the prototype of the scientist; a hero but a hero damned, chained on the Caucasus with vultures gnawing at his liver." Ambition to make our existence easier, to bend "nature", and to tame magic. Know-how vs know-what. These vultures aren’t picking on the liver of Prometheus. This creature isn’t chasing Frankenstein. The punishment is falling on us all, but mostly on the less privileged and most vulnerable.
4. The Other The impulse, the uncontrollable, billocation, transmutation, doppelganger. ? But “rule-bending” in this sense is part of human nature, part of how we define consciousness and distinguish human from animal. This ambition to make existence easier is probably the survival tactic that made humanity thrive. By inventing technology to remove obstacles (machine = remedy), we divert our efforts to other areas. Technology as an integral part of being human.
